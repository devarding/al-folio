I am a Postdoctoral Research Staff Member at the Climate Analysis Group, Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory, United States. I worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Earth System Science Interdisciplinary Center, University of Maryland, College Park. I received my Ph.D. in Atmospheric Sciences in 2021 from the University of Maryland, College Park, focusing on remote sensing of aerosol and the planetary boundary layer, and exploring their interactions.

My research is deeply rooted in the study of convective systems, quintessential examples of chaos in our real world. These chaotic systems are inherently linked with three key components: the planetary boundary layer (PBL), clouds, and the land surface. I perceive the interactions between clouds and land surface not as isolated phenomena but as parts of a cohesive system, with the PBL serving as the essential medium facilitating their dialogue. Delving into the details of how the PBL mediates this dialogue is central to my work, aiming to unravel the complexities of convection patterns and the coupling between clouds and the land surface. PBL turbulence can catalyze this coupling processes and induce rapid and seemingly random vertical movements of aerosols and humidity within this coupled system.

My research employs multi-scale modeling techniques, including large-eddy simulations and kilometer-scale storm-resolving models, alongside advanced remote sensing technologies like lidar and radar, complemented by cutting-edge deep learning methodologies. By leveraging real-world observations, my work aims to enhance the representation of the surface-PBL-cloud system in climate models and enrich our comprehension of convective system amidst climate change.

Building on this overarching goal of understanding the integrated surface-PBL-cloud system, I have developed several interconnected research topics, including:

  • Remote Sensing of the PBL and Cloud-Land Coupling
  • The Role of Aerosol in Surface-PBL-Cloud System
  • Process-level Mechanisms of Cloud-Land Coupling
  • The Representation of Cloud Coupling in Reanalysis Data and Multiscale Models
  • Deep Learning Driven Simulation of Surface-PBL-Cloud System
